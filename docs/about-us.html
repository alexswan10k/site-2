Our process

First we will sit down with you and attempt to understand the problem you are trying to solve,and help guide you to a solution that actually helps solve this. Some customers have a clear idea of what they want, some need help here. It is our goal at this stage to ensure that you do not waste time and money trying to build the wrong thing.

We may also recommend a UX research phase here, to try to better understand your problem, and further refine your proposed solution.

Next, we have two options depending on the flexibility of the customer:

The agile approach

Where more flexible Time & Materials arrangements are possible, we absolutely recommend following agile principles throughout the build of your application, which almost always leads to better results all round, happier customers, and less resources wasted building unimportant features.

Where applicable, we begin by wireframing your app, mapping the journeys, and getting feedback from your users as soon as possible. Once this is complete, this small slice of the appliction is built, deployed, and put into the hands of users as fast as possible. As users find issues and suggest improvements, these can quickly be fed back into the process, and used to shape the next iteration of changes.

This process generally has a short iteration time (usually around 2-3 weeks, although initial setup may take longer), and can be repeated as many times as you see fit. We can also arrange regular post-sprint demo's for your team if required.

The great benefit of this approach is you are always focused building only what adds the most value to your application at any given time, and progress is continuously visible. You can choose to pause or resume development at any time, without having to commit large sums of money up front.

The up-front approach

Although not generally recommended, we do appreciate that customers sometimes have fixed budgets to work with, or wish to know up front exactly how much something is going to cost. The reality is estimating software is extremely difficult, and as such, there is a significant premium for the added risk we are taking on.

When we have an idea what the solution looks like, we attempt to break down the solution into a number of finite building blocks of understandable complexity (pages, components, integration points, etc). Each element is assigned a value for complexity and risk. We also build in some contingency for scope creep due to missing requirements, and any unknown unknowns. This is all used to understand the scope, and to create an esimate of the resources required. Here we also feed in your requirements for any design work, wireframing, and testing, should these be knowable up front.

When all required constructs are known and agreed on, we will attempt to compartmentalise vertical slices of functionality, and begin construction one slice at a time if architecture permits this. This then allows us to focus on early testing, delivery, and user feedback for each slice, rather than a far more risky big-bang approach.

Concluding the project

When all requirements are met, we can also provide a hand-over to concerned parties, along with any system configuration where applicable. In the event that we arrange to host or manage your software, we can provide ongoing maintenance contracts to provide support and ongoing bugfixes.

As we are fully aware that software is rarely ever "finished", we are always happy to help with change requests, although these will need to be separately estimated and scheduled.
